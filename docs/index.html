What is the Smart Contact Mic? ​

The Soniphorm Smart Contact Mic (SCM) is a contact microphone with a programmable DSP engine built into the board. Clip it to a wine glass, a spring, a radiator, a window, or your own body, and it listens to vibrations the way any contact mic does. The difference is what happens next: the signal is fed into a patch you design visually in a browser-based patcher, and from there it can drive anything. Every tap can trigger an oscillator, a drum machine, a sampler, or a MIDI note to an external synth. Every sound can be looped, sliced, granulated, resampled, or sequenced in time with the rest of the patch.

Because the SCM is reprogrammable, one device can be many things -- a sensitive instrument pickup, a field recorder, an improvised looper, a step sequencer driven by real-world taps, a MIDI trigger for a DAW, a granular sketchpad, or any combination of those at once.

How It Works ​

  1. Open the patcher in your browser at soniphorm.com/patcher
  2. Add modules from the palette -- oscillators, filters, effects, controls
  3. Connect them by dragging cables between ports
  4. Upload to your SCM over USB
  5. Play -- drive the patch from the patcher over USB, or from an Android phone over Bluetooth MIDI
