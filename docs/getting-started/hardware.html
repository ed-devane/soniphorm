The Hardware

On this page

The Hardware ​

The Smart Contact Mic is a minimal board with no physical controls on-board. All parameter control happens virtually -- from the patcher app or over Bluetooth MIDI.

Virtual Controls ​

The SCM has no physical pots, buttons, or encoder. Patches are programmed and controlled in two ways, depending on whether you're on a computer or a mobile device:

  • Patcher app, over USB -- on a desktop or laptop, soniphorm.com/patcher is the browser-based control surface. Connect the SCM over USB and the patcher's on-screen MIDI control strip drives the current patch live. This is the primary control path when you're tethered to a computer.
  • Bluetooth MIDI, on mobile -- BLE MIDI is the SCM's built-in control path for mobile devices. Pair your phone or tablet with the SCM over Bluetooth and use any standard BLE MIDI controller app to send CCs and notes. Currently supported on Android only; iOS support is in development.

Either way, the underlying control surface is the same: four virtual pots and four virtual buttons, organised into six colour-coded banks. The next section explains how that works.

The portable rig ​

The SCM is designed to disappear into your pocket. The intended portable setup is:

  1. Plug the SCM into your phone with a USB-C cable -- the phone powers the SCM directly.
  2. Pair the SCM with the same phone over Bluetooth.
  3. Open a BLE MIDI controller app on the phone and play.

No laptop, no extra battery, no cables to anything else. Phone + SCM + a contact mic clipped to whatever you're listening to.

Banking System ​

The SCM organises its virtual control surface into six colour-coded banks. Each bank is a set of 8 MIDI CC slots (4 pots + 4 buttons) wired to module parameters in your patch. Switching banks remaps those same 8 CCs to a different set of parameters, so one patch can address many parameters from a compact control surface like a four-fader BLE MIDI app on a phone.

For the full banking workflow (why it's structured this way, soft takeover, auto-mapped modules, manual Pot/LEDButton modules), see the Banks and Controls guide.

2Green

Switching Banks ​

Two ways:

  • In the patcher: the canvas has a row of bank buttons (1-6) and left/right arrows. Click the bank you want.
  • Over BLE MIDI: send CC 48 with values 0-5 to select banks 1-6. Useful for hands-free switching from a phone, tablet, or any BLE MIDI controller.

Virtual Control Surface ​

The patcher canvas includes a vertical MIDI control strip with four on-screen faders (the "pots") and four on-screen buttons. Drag a fader or click a button to send the corresponding MIDI CC to the SCM live -- this is the equivalent of turning a knob on the Eurorack version. The faders and buttons follow whichever bank you're currently on.

You can also drop Pot and LEDButton modules onto the canvas itself if you want named, per-parameter controls placed near the modules they drive. Each Pot/LEDButton module has a bank parameter in the inspector.

MIDI CC Reference ​

When you want to drive the SCM from a BLE MIDI controller app on your phone or tablet, these are the CCs to map:

Pots, bank 2CC 24-27
Pots, bank 3CC 28-31
Pots, bank 4CC 32-35
Pots, bank 5CC 36-39
Pots, bank 6CC 40-43
Buttons (all banks)CC 44-47

Buttons live on the same four CCs across every bank. If you want a button to do different things in different banks, wire it to different module inputs in the patch and the bank context decides which one is active.

Soft Takeover ​

When you switch banks, parameters do not jump to the new bank's stored values. Instead, the value stays where it is until an incoming pot CC crosses within 5% of the new bank's stored value, at which point the control "takes over" again. This prevents abrupt parameter jumps -- e.g. a filter cutoff suddenly snapping wide open -- when you change context on a controller that has its own physical knob positions.

Auto-Mapped Modules ​

Some modules (Stereo Mixer, Drum Machine, BPM Looper, Multi Sample, PortaSampler, and others) automatically claim one to three banks and pre-assign all their pots and buttons. These modules show a coloured footer on the canvas indicating which bank(s) they own, and the patcher inspector lists what each pot and button in each bank does for that module.

Pot and LEDButton modules you place manually get assigned to the next free bank. The bank assignment for any module is editable in the inspector.

Audio ​

The SCM is designed for contact mic applications. A built-in differential piezo preamp feeds a mono codec that handles both input and output, and an onboard speaker amplifier drives small speakers directly from the board.

Power ​

The board is powered over USB-C. Anything that delivers regulated 5V over USB will do: a laptop port, a wall adapter, a USB power bank, or a phone with USB-C OTG output. The phone-powered case is fully supported -- it's the basis of the portable rig described above. Avoid unpowered USB hubs for best stability.

SD Card ​

A micro SD card slot provides storage for samples, recordings, and multi-sample banks. Format the card as FAT32.
